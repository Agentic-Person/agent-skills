## 1. Core Philosophy & Mental Model

### The Two Pillars of Modern SEO

All SEO activity reduces to two core activities:

1. **Produce the Right Content** — Create specific, entity-matched pages that prove to Google and AI systems exactly what services you offer, in exactly which locations, with genuine expertise.
2. **Build Trust** — Accumulate external signals (citations, backlinks, reviews, schema, consistency) so that algorithms (Google, ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) believe you are who you say you are.

Every tactic in this skill serves one or both of these pillars.

### How Modern Google Works (Transformer Technology)

Google has used transformer-based AI (the same technology as ChatGPT — which Google invented in 2017) since approximately 2019. This means:

- Google does NOT scan pages for keyword matches the way it did pre-2015
- Google processes and UNDERSTANDS content as an AI would — it recognizes entities, intent, and context
- Traditional SEO metrics (keyword density, domain authority, keyword difficulty scores) are largely irrelevant for LOCAL service businesses
- What matters is: Does your content clearly and specifically describe a recognized service entity in a specific location?
- Google renders pages visually (using mobile Chrome) — it does NOT read raw HTML source code the way old crawlers did

### Local SEO vs. National SEO — Critical Distinction

| Factor | Local SEO | National/E-commerce SEO |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------|
| Primary goal | Rank GBP in map pack + local organic | Rank website in organic only |
| Domain authority | Mostly irrelevant | Significant factor |
| Keyword difficulty | Mostly irrelevant | Major factor |
| Backlink volume | Quality > Quantity | High volume matters |
| Content depth | Service entities + city | Topic clusters + long-tail |
| Competition | Local businesses | National brands + aggregators |
| Win condition | Top 3 map pack + page 1 organic | Page 1 organic position 1-5 |

### The Three Google Local Ranking Factors

Google officially uses three factors to rank local businesses:

1. **Proximity** — How close is the business to the searcher? (You cannot control this)
2. **Relevance** — How well does your GBP and website describe your specific services? (Controlled by content)
3. **Authority** — How much does the external web trust your business? (Controlled by citations, links, reviews)

Strategy: Since proximity is fixed, all effort goes into maximizing relevance and authority.

### The Local Rank Map Mental Model

Imagine your service area as a grid of 169 points (13x13). At each point, someone is searching for your service. The **Local Rank Map** (tools: Lead Snap, BrightLocal, Local Falcon) shows your rank at every point. The key metric is:

**Top 3%** = Percentage of those 169 points where you rank in positions 1-3 (the green zone)

- Top 3 positions capture ~75% of clicks in local search
- Below position 3 = effectively invisible for that location point
- Your entire content strategy is driven by improving this percentage

## 2. The Core 30 Framework

### What Is the Core 30?

The Core 30 is the foundational website architecture for any local contractor. It consists of **30 dedicated, well-structured pages** — one for every category and service listed on your Google Business Profile (GBP). The number 30 is a target; actual count depends on trade complexity.

**Core 30 Hierarchy:**
```
Homepage (Primary GBP Category + City)
├── Category Page 1 (Secondary Category + City)
│   ├── Service Page 1.1 (Service + City)
│   ├── Service Page 1.2 (Service + City)
│   └── Service Page 1.3 (Service + City)
├── Category Page 2 (Secondary Category + City)
│   ├── Service Page 2.1 (Service + City)
│   ├── Service Page 2.2 (Service + City)
│   └── Service Page 2.3 (Service + City)
├── Category Page 3 (Secondary Category + City)
│   └── ...
└── Category Page 4 (Secondary Category + City)
    └── ...
```

### Why Core 30 Works

- Each page targets a SEPARATE service entity that Google recognizes as distinct
- Google has separate map pack SERPs for "roofer Houston" vs. "roof repair Houston" vs. "roof replacement Houston" — these are different entities with different ranking opportunities
- Having one page per entity gives you 30 opportunities to appear in top results instead of 1
- The hierarchy mirrors your GBP structure exactly, reinforcing relevance signals
- Internal linking flows authority from homepage → category pages → service pages

### Core 30 Page Requirements

Each page in the Core 30 must have:
- [ ] Unique title tag (Category/Service + City + Brand)
- [ ] Matching H1 (Category/Service + City)
- [ ] Minimum 500-800 words of original, specific content
- [ ] At least 2-3 internal links (to parent and sibling pages)
- [ ] At least 1 external link pointing to it (backlink)
- [ ] Correct schema markup (LocalBusiness + Service)
- [ ] Relevant images with alt text
- [ ] Call-to-action (phone number, contact form)
- [ ] Mobile-responsive design

### Core 30 Internal Linking Rules

1. **Editorial links only** — Links must be embedded within paragraph text, not just navigation or footer
2. **Navigation counts minimally** — Nav and footer links pass little authority
3. **Anchor text must be descriptive** — Use "roof repair in Houston" not "click here"
4. **Link UP and DOWN the hierarchy** — Service pages link to their category page; category pages link to homepage and to their service pages
5. **Cross-link related services** — "Hail damage repair" page should link to "storm damage" category and "roof inspection" service

## 3. Google Business Profile (GBP) Mastery

### Why GBP Is Primary for Local SEO

For local contractor searches, the **Map Pack (3 GBP listings)** appears above all organic results and captures the majority of clicks. Your GBP IS your primary local search asset — the website supports and extends it.

Google uses your GBP to understand:
- What services you offer
- Where you operate
- How trustworthy/popular you are
- What customers say about you

### GBP Complete Setup Checklist

**Business Information:**
- [ ] Business name exactly matches website and all citations (no keyword stuffing in name)
- [ ] Primary category = your most important service (e.g., "Roofing Contractor")
- [ ] Secondary categories selected (3-5, not the maximum 10 — quality over quantity)
- [ ] Service area defined (radius or list of zip codes/cities)
- [ ] Phone number matches website and all citations exactly
- [ ] Address matches website and all citations exactly (including abbreviations — choose St. or Street and be consistent EVERYWHERE)
- [ ] Website URL added
- [ ] Business hours fully filled out (including holiday hours)
- [ ] Business description written (750 character max — mention primary service, city, key differentiators)

**Services Section:**
- [ ] Every service listed with its own name AND description (100-300 words per service)
- [ ] Services mirror your website service pages exactly
- [ ] Service descriptions are specific, not generic
- [ ] Pricing information added where applicable

**Photos (Minimum 20, Target 50+):**
- [ ] Exterior photo (team, truck, job site)
- [ ] Interior/workspace photos
- [ ] In-progress work photos
- [ ] Completed project photos (before/after preferred)
- [ ] Team member photos
- [ ] Logo uploaded to logo section
- [ ] Cover photo selected (high quality, action shot)
- [ ] Phone photos preferred (automatically geotagged to your service area)
- [ ] New photos added monthly minimum

**Attributes:**
- [ ] All applicable attributes checked
- [ ] "Licensed," "Insured," "Free estimates" if applicable
- [ ] Accessibility attributes
- [ ] Service options (online estimates, on-site service, etc.)

**Q&A Section:**
- [ ] Add your own Q&As (you can post questions AND answers)
- [ ] Cover common customer questions
- [ ] Answer any user-submitted questions within 24 hours

**Ongoing Activity (Weekly/Monthly):**
- [ ] Post at minimum 1x per week (offers, updates, completed projects)
- [ ] Respond to ALL reviews within 24-48 hours
- [ ] Upload new photos 2-4x per month
- [ ] Monitor and respond to Q&A
- [ ] Use GBP management software for automation where possible

### GBP Category Strategy

**Primary Category Selection (Critical):**
- Choose the category that describes what you ARE, not just what you do
- Roofing company → "Roofing Contractor" (not "Roofing Supply Store")
- HVAC company → "HVAC Contractor" (not "Air Conditioning Store")
- This single selection has the highest weight in Google's local algorithm

**Secondary Category Best Practices:**
- Select 3-5 secondary categories (Google allows 10 but more ≠ better)
- Each secondary category = a separate ranking opportunity AND a required website page
- Common secondary categories for roofers:
  - "Insulation Contractor" (if offered)
  - "Gutter Installation Service"
  - "Siding Contractor" (if offered)
  - "Storm Damage Restoration Service"

### GBP vs. Website Consistency — Non-Negotiable

Every piece of information on your GBP must match your website EXACTLY:

| Element | GBP | Website Must Match |
|---------|-----|--------------------|
| Business name | ABC Roofing LLC | ABC Roofing LLC |
| Address | 123 Main St | 123 Main St (not 123 Main Street) |
| Phone | (713) 555-1234 | (713) 555-1234 |
| Categories | Roofing Contractor | Homepage targets "Roofing Contractor" |
| Services | Roof Repair, Roof Replacement... | Dedicated pages for each |

Mismatches confuse Google and reduce trust for both Google and AI systems.

### GBP Posts — Content Strategy

**Post Types & When to Use:**
1. **Offer posts** — Limited-time discount, free inspection promotion
2. **Update posts** — Completed project showcase, new service announcement
3. **Event posts** — Community involvement, trade shows
4. **Product posts** — Specific materials/brands you use (e.g., GAF shingles)

**Post Best Practices:**
- Use real project photos (before/after)
- Mention the neighborhood or area (signals location relevance)
- Include a CTA button (Call Now, Book Online, Learn More)
- Keep text between 150-300 words
- Post at least 1x per week consistently
- Automate with GBP management software to maintain consistency

## 4. Service Entity Research (Replaces Keyword Research)

### Why Traditional Keyword Research Is Dead for Local SEO

Traditional keyword research tools (Ahrefs, SEMrush, Moz) provide:
- Keyword difficulty scores
- Monthly search volume
- Domain authority comparisons

For LOCAL service businesses, these metrics are largely irrelevant because:
1. Google uses AI (transformers) to understand ENTITIES, not keywords
2. A "roofing contractor" page and a "roof replacement" page are DIFFERENT ENTITIES in Google's model — they generate separate map pack SERPs with different businesses ranking
3. Local search intent is hyperlocal — volume numbers are tiny but conversion intent is extreme
4. The competitive landscape is other local contractors, not national brands

**Traditional keyword research takes 3 hours and produces inferior targeting.**
**Service entity research takes 30 minutes and produces superior targeting.**

### Service Entity Research — Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Primary Entity Validation**
```
1. Open Google in incognito/private mode
2. Type: [Primary Category] [City Name] (e.g., "roofing contractor Houston")
3. Note: Who appears in the map pack?
4. Note: What organic results appear?
5. Validate that this is the correct primary entity for your GBP and homepage
```

**Step 2: Secondary Entity Discovery**
```
1. Type each potential service separately:
   - "roof repair Houston"
   - "roof replacement Houston"
   - "roof installation Houston"
   - "metal roofing Houston"
   - "hail damage roof Houston"
2. CRITICAL OBSERVATION: Different businesses appear in each map pack
3. This PROVES these are separate entities in Google's model
4. Each distinct map pack = a separate page needed on your website
5. Document every service that generates its own distinct SERP
```

**Step 3: People Also Ask (PAA) Mining**
```
1. Search your primary entity ("roofing contractor Houston")
2. Scroll to People Also Ask section
3. Click to expand each question — this generates MORE related questions
4. Keep clicking — you can generate 30-50+ questions
5. These questions = supporting content page topics
6. Example questions:
   - "How much does roof replacement cost in Houston?"
   - "How long does a roof last in Texas?"
   - "Does homeowners insurance cover roof replacement?"
   - "How do I know if I need a new roof?"
7. Export or document all questions — these become FAQ pages and supporting content
```

**Step 4: Entity Validation Checklist**
```
For each potential entity/service page:
[ ] Does it generate its own distinct map pack in Google?
[ ] Do different businesses rank for it vs. your primary entity?
[ ] Does your GBP have this as a listed service or category?
[ ] Is it a real distinct service (not just a keyword variation)?
[ ] Would a customer specifically search for this term?

If YES to 3+ above → Create a dedicated service page for it
```

**Step 5: Competitor Gap Analysis**
```
1. Search each entity keyword
2. Click the top-ranking map pack business
3. Go to their website
4. Note what service pages they have that you're missing
5. Note their page structure and content depth
6. Identify gaps where you can provide MORE specific, better content
```

## 5. On-Page SEO — Complete Guide

### Title Tag Optimization

**Formula:**
```
[Primary Keyword (Service + City)] [Differentiator] | [Brand Name]
```

**Good Examples:**
- "Houston Roofing Contractor — Same Day Estimates | ABC Roofing"
- "Roof Replacement Houston TX | Licensed & Insured | ABC Roofing"
- "Emergency HVAC Repair Denver | 24/7 Service | Cool Air Co."

**Bad Examples (Avoid):**
- "Home" (60%+ of local sites use this — easiest win to fix)
- "ABC Roofing LLC" (brand only — misses all keyword opportunity)
- "Best Houston Roofer" ("best" is spammy per modern Google)
- "#1 Roofing Company in Houston" (superlatives = spammy signals)

**Title Tag Rules:**
- Include primary GBP category AND city name — both required
- Keep under 60 characters (or it gets truncated in SERPs)
- Avoid adjectives like "best," "top," "greatest" — Google' algorithm flags these
- Each page must have a UNIQUE title tag — no duplicates across site
- Homepage title = primary category + city
- Category pages = category name + city
- Service pages = service name + city

### H1 Tag (Primary Heading)

**Rules:**
- Exactly ONE H1 per page
- Must include the same primary keyword as the title tag
- Unlike title tag, H1 is user-facing — should also speak to humans
- Example: "Roofing Contractor Houston — Protecting Homes Since 2008"
- Must match the title tag thematically (same entity + city)

### H2 Tags (Secondary Headings)

**On the Homepage:**
- Each secondary GBP category gets its own H2
- Under each H2: 50-100 words describing the category
- Content under H2 must cover: What this category is, why customers need it, what specific services fall under it
- H2 followed by editorial link to that category's dedicated page

**Example Homepage H2 Structure (Roofing):**
```
H1: Houston Roofing Contractor — Expert Roof Services Since 2008

H2: Residential Roofing in Houston
[50-100 words describing residential roofing services...]
[→ Learn more about our Residential Roofing services]

H2: Storm Damage Roof Repair
[50-100 words about storm damage services...]
[→ See our Storm Damage Repair process]

H2: Commercial Roofing Houston
[50-100 words about commercial services...]
[→ View Commercial Roofing options]

H2: Gutter Installation & Repair
[50-100 words about gutters...]
[→ Explore Gutter Services]
```

**On Category Pages:**
- H1 = Category name + City
- H2 tags = Each individual service under that category
- Each H2 service section: 100-200 words + link to that service's page

**On Service Pages:**
- H1 = Service name + City
- H2 tags = Subtopics: process overview, common problems, FAQ, why choose us, service area
- H3 tags = Individual FAQ questions, specific sub-processes

### Meta Descriptions

**Formula:**
```
[Service] in [City] — [Key Differentiator]. [Social Proof]. [CTA with phone number].
```

**Example:**
```
Roof replacement in Houston by licensed, insured contractors. GAF Master Elite certified. 
Free same-day estimates. Call (713) 555-1234.
```

**Rules:**
- Keep under 155 characters
- Include primary keyword and city
- Include a phone number (clickable on mobile)
- Include a differentiator (certified, 20+ years, same-day, 24/7)
- Google does NOT use meta description as a ranking signal — it's for click-through rate (CTR)
- Every page must have a UNIQUE meta description

### Content Body — Writing Requirements

**Three-Audience Rule:**
Every page must work for three readers simultaneously:
1. **Google's Algorithm** — Entity clarity, internal links, schema, proper structure
2. **AI Systems** — Explicit labeling, schema, clear topic sentences, factual specificity
3. **Human Users** — Readable, specific, locally relevant, builds trust, drives calls

**Content Length Guidelines:**
- Homepage: 800-1,500 words
- Category pages: 600-1,200 words
- Service pages: 500-1,000 words
- Supporting content (FAQ, blog): 800-2,000 words
- Geographic location pages: 400-800 words

**Required Content Elements per Service Page:**
- [ ] What the service is (definition/overview)
- [ ] Why the customer needs it (pain points, urgency)
- [ ] How your company performs it (process overview — 4-7 steps)
- [ ] What makes your approach/company different
- [ ] Local specificity (Houston weather, Texas building codes, etc.)
- [ ] Social proof (number of jobs, years in business, certifications)
- [ ] FAQ section (3-7 questions from Google PAA)
- [ ] Clear CTAs (phone number, contact form, free estimate button)
- [ ] Internal links to related services
- [ ] Images (before/after, team, equipment) with descriptive alt text

**Content Formatting Best Practices:**
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences maximum)
- Bullet points for lists of features, steps, services
- Numbered lists for process steps
- Bold key terms on first use
- Tables for comparisons (e.g., material types, pricing tiers)
- Pull quotes or callout boxes for key stats
- One image per 200-300 words minimum

**Content Specificity — Critical:**
Generic content = low rankings. Specific content = higher rankings.

BAD: "We provide quality roofing services for homes in the area."
GOOD: "We replace roofs on Houston's older Craftsman homes in Montrose and Heights neighborhoods using CertainTeed Landmark shingles, rated for 130 mph wind resistance — essential for hurricane season preparation."

### What NOT to Do — On-Page Pitfalls

**Collapsed/Accordion Content:**
- Google renders pages like a mobile browser — it cannot CLICK to open accordions
- Any content hidden behind a collapse/expand toggle is largely invisible to Googlebot
- Either expand all accordions by default OR convert them to visible content sections
- FAQ sections especially: Use visible Q&A with proper schema, not hidden accordions

**Animated Hero Images:**
- Auto-playing sliders and animated hero images cause higher bounce rates
- Users bounce before the page settles
- Use a single, static hero image with clear headline and CTA

**Duplicate Content:**
- Using identical or near-identical content across multiple pages
- Common trap: Franchise/chain websites that use the same template for all locations
- Each page must have ORIGINAL, SPECIFIC content — not "Houston" swapped in for "Dallas"
- Duplicate content actively suppresses rankings across all affected pages

**Keyword Stuffing:**
- Repeating keywords unnaturally
- Google's AI reads like a human — keyword stuffing is detected and penalized
- Write naturally, mentioning the service and city as needed for clarity

## 6. Technical SEO

### Mobile-First Design — Non-Negotiable

**Critical Understanding:** Google crawls websites using the most recent version of **mobile Chrome** (Googlebot Smartphone). It does NOT crawl your desktop site. This means:

- Design mobile first, then verify desktop
- If it looks perfect on desktop but breaks on mobile → Google sees the broken version
- Test with Google Search Console > URL Inspection > View Rendered Page
- Test with Chrome DevTools mobile emulator

**Mobile Design Requirements:**
- [ ] Tap targets (buttons, links) minimum 44x44px
- [ ] Text readable without zooming (minimum 16px body)
- [ ] No horizontal scrolling
- [ ] Phone number is click-to-call
- [ ] Contact form works on mobile
- [ ] Images properly sized for mobile (not oversized)
- [ ] Navigation accessible via hamburger menu
- [ ] Page loads in under 3 seconds on 4G connection

### Core Web Vitals

Google's page experience ranking factors:

| Metric | What It Measures | Good Score | How to Improve |
|--------|-----------------|------------|----------------|
| LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) | How fast main content loads | < 2.5 seconds | Optimize hero image, use WebP format, CDN |
| CLS (Cumulative Layout Shift) | Visual stability (no jumping) | < 0.1 | Set image dimensions, avoid dynamic content inserts |
| INP (Interaction to Next Paint) | Response to user interaction | < 200ms | Minimize JavaScript, defer non-critical scripts |

**Tools to measure:**
- Google Search Console → Core Web Vitals report
- PageSpeed Insights (pagespeed.web.dev)
- GTmetrix
- WebPageTest.org

### Site Architecture Requirements

**Required Files:**
```
robots.txt     — Controls what Googlebot can/cannot crawl
sitemap.xml    — Lists all pages for Google to index
```

**robots.txt basics:**
```
User-agent: *
Disallow: /admin/
Disallow: /wp-admin/
Allow: /
Sitemap: https://yourdomain.com/sitemap.xml
```

**sitemap.xml requirements:**
- List all important pages
- Keep URLs < 50,000 per sitemap
- Update automatically when new pages are added (most CMS do this)
- Submit in Google Search Console

### Crawlability — The Googlebot Cannot Click Rule

Google renders pages visually but cannot:
- Click dropdown menus to expand
- Open accordion/collapse sections
- Interact with JavaScript-heavy content
- Fill forms
- Scroll (by default in older crawls)

**Solutions:**
- Make all important content visible on page load (no clicks required)
- Use static HTML for navigation where possible
- Test by disabling JavaScript — all important content should still be visible
- Use Google Search Console URL Inspection to see rendered version

### URL Structure

**Best Practice URL Patterns:**
```
https://domain.com/                          → Homepage
https://domain.com/residential-roofing/     → Category
https://domain.com/residential-roofing/roof-replacement/  → Service (with parent)
https://domain.com/roof-replacement-houston/ → Alternative flat structure
https://domain.com/locations/memorial-heights/ → Location page
https://domain.com/blog/how-long-does-roof-last/ → Supporting content
```

**URL Rules:**
- All lowercase
- Hyphens (not underscores) between words
- Include primary keyword in URL
- Short as possible while remaining descriptive
- No dates in URLs for evergreen service pages
- 301 redirect old URLs when changing structure

### Site Speed Optimization

**Image Optimization (Most Common Speed Issue):**
- Convert all images to WebP format
- Compress images before uploading (tools: Squoosh, TinyPNG)
- Serve correctly sized images (do not upload 4000px image for 400px display)
- Use lazy loading for below-fold images (loading="lazy" attribute)
- Set explicit width and height attributes to prevent CLS

**JavaScript Optimization:**
- Defer non-critical JavaScript
- Remove unused JavaScript (check with Coverage tab in Chrome DevTools)
- Use modern frameworks that support server-side rendering (Next.js)

**Hosting Requirements:**
- Use reputable hosting with <200ms server response time (TTFB)
- Use a CDN for static assets (Cloudflare free plan is sufficient for most local sites)
- Enable browser caching
- Enable GZIP/Brotli compression

### HTTPS — Required

- All pages must be served over HTTPS
- No mixed content (HTTP resources on HTTPS page)
- Redirect all HTTP → HTTPS with 301
- Redirect non-www → www (or vice versa) consistently

### Technical Crawling with Screaming Frog

**Screaming Frog** (free for up to 500 URLs) is the primary technical audit tool:

**Step-by-Step Technical Audit:**
```
1. Download Screaming Frog (screamingfrog.co.uk/seo-spider)
2. Enter client website URL and run crawl
3. Export all URLs to spreadsheet
4. Filter by: 4XX errors (broken pages), 3XX redirects, duplicate titles, duplicate H1s
5. Check: Missing title tags, missing H1s, missing meta descriptions
6. Check: Orphaned pages (no internal links pointing to them)
7. Export internal links report
8. Feed errors to Claude or ChatGPT for fix instructions
9. Implement fixes and re-crawl to verify
```

**Priority Issues to Fix:**
1. 404 errors (broken pages)
2. Duplicate title tags
3. Missing title tags
4. Duplicate H1 tags or missing H1s
5. Redirect chains (A→B→C instead of A→C)
6. Orphaned pages (not linked from anywhere)
7. Crawl depth > 3 clicks from homepage

- **Google** (25 years of crawling experience) can often understand content without schema, but schema accelerates and improves accuracy

- Schema is therefore MORE important for AEO than for traditional SEO

## 8. Local SEO Strategy

### The Local SEO Ecosystem Map

```
                    LOCAL SEARCH VISIBILITY
                           |
           ________________________________
          |                |               |
      MAP PACK          LOCAL            AI
      RANKINGS          ORGANIC        ANSWERS
          |                |               |
    GBP Authority    Website SEO    Brand Mentions
    Citations        Content        Schema
    Reviews          Links          Consistency
    Proximity        E-E-A-T        Citations
```

All three channels feed from the same trust signals. Optimizing one benefits all three.

### The Three Pillars of Local Ranking — Expanded

#### Pillar 1: Proximity (Cannot Control)
- Google uses the searcher's physical location
- A roofer in north Houston will never rank #1 for someone searching in south Houston from 30 miles away
- Strategy: Accept proximity limits and maximize relevance + authority in your core service area
- Rank map analysis reveals your true proximity coverage

#### Pillar 2: Relevance (Content Controls This)
Relevance signals Google uses:
- GBP primary and secondary categories
- GBP services listed and described
- Website content matching GBP categories
- Dedicated page per service entity
- On-page keyword signals (titles, H1s, body)
- Schema markup explicitly labeling services
- Internal linking structure

**How to Maximize Relevance:**
1. Every GBP category = a page on your website
2. Every GBP service = a page on your website
3. Page content matches the service entity exactly
4. Title tags and H1s match GBP category/service names
5. Schema markup confirms the service entity

#### Pillar 3: Authority (External Signals Control This)
Authority signals Google uses:
- Number and quality of citations
- Super citations (verified high-authority platforms)
- Backlinks from external sites
- Local authority links (chamber, sponsorships)
- Number and quality of Google reviews
- Review recency and response rate
- GBP activity (posting frequency, photo uploads)
- Social media signals (secondary)

### Local Citation Strategy

**What Is a Citation?**
Any online mention of your business NAP (Name, Address, Phone). Citations teach search engines that your business is real, established, and legitimate.

**Citation Tiers:**

Tier 1 — Core Data Aggregators (Must have, highest impact):
- Google Business Profile
- Apple Maps / Yelp (via Localeze/Data Axle)
- Bing for Business
- Foursquare (feeds dozens of downstream directories)

Tier 2 — Industry-Specific Directories:
- Angi (formerly Angie's List)
- HomeAdvisor
- Thumbtack
- Houzz (for contractors)
- Porch.com
- BuildZoom
- Contractor.com
- GAF/CertainTeed/manufacturer dealer finders (for roofers)
- ACCA directory (for HVAC)

Tier 3 — General Business Directories:
- Yellow Pages (yellowpages.com)
- Manta
- Superpages
- Citysearch
- MerchantCircle
- EZLocal
- Local.com

Tier 4 — Local/Regional:
- Local Chamber of Commerce directory
- City government contractor directory
- Local newspaper business directory
- State licensing board directory
- Better Business Bureau

**Citation Consistency Rules — Non-Negotiable:**

Pick ONE format for each element and use it EVERYWHERE:
```
Business Name: ABC Roofing LLC       (not "ABC Roofing" or "A.B.C. Roofing")
Address: 123 Main St                 (not "123 Main Street" or "123 Main")
City: Houston                        (not "Houston, TX" in some, "Houston" in others)
Phone: (713) 555-1234               (not "713-555-1234" or "7135551234")
```

Inconsistency = confusion for Google = suppressed rankings.

**Citation Audit Process:**
```
1. Use BrightLocal Citation Finder or Whitespark to discover existing citations
2. Export all found citations to spreadsheet
3. Identify inconsistencies (wrong phone, old address, wrong name format)
4. Correct each one manually (most directories allow free edits)
5. Build missing citations in priority order (Tier 1 first)
6. Monitor quarterly for new incorrect citations
```

### NAP Consistency Across the Entire Web

Every location your business appears online must have identical NAP:
- GBP
- Website (footer, contact page, schema)
- All citations
- Social media profiles
- Industry directories
- Press releases
- Email signatures
- Invoices and documents (these get scanned/indexed)

### Local Content Strategy — Beyond the Core 30

**Phase 1: Core 30 (Foundation — months 1-3)**
Build all Core 30 pages. No other content until this is complete.

**Phase 2: Supporting Content (months 3-6, if rank map < threshold)**
Build pages answering specific FAQs identified from Google PAA:
- "How much does roof replacement cost in Houston?"
- "Does homeowners insurance cover roof damage in Texas?"
- "How long should a roof last in Houston's climate?"
- "What are signs I need a new roof?"
- "Best roofing materials for Texas heat?"
- "How to file a roof insurance claim in Texas?"

These pages:
- Target informational intent (not commercial)
- Build topical authority
- Feed internal links to service pages
- Answer the exact questions AI systems get asked
- Each needs its own external backlink

**Phase 3: Geographic Content (months 6+, when rank map ≥ threshold)**
Only after demonstrating topical authority, create location pages:
- "Roofing Contractor in Memorial Heights"
- "Roof Repair in Katy TX"
- "Roofing Company Serving The Woodlands"

Location page content must be UNIQUE — not a template with city name swapped:
- Mention local landmarks, neighborhoods, local weather patterns
- Include local stats (population, housing age, common roof types)
- Reference local building codes or permits
- Add maps and local-specific photos
- At minimum 400-600 words of unique content per location

## 9. Link Building for Local Contractors

### Why Links Still Matter

After AI-generated content flooded the internet (late 2023 onward), Google needed a quality signal that AI couldn't easily fake. Links became MORE important, not less, because:
1. Links from real sites require real human decisions
2. AI can write content but can't build real relationships
3. Google uses links to distinguish genuine expertise from AI spam

### The Two-Category Link Framework

**Category 1: Content Validation Links ("Not AI Slop" Links)**

Purpose: Prove your content is real, non-spammy, and worth indexing

Characteristics:
- Do NOT need to be local
- Do NOT need high domain authority
- DO need to come from real, non-spammy sites
- One per page is sufficient
- Target: 30 links for Core 30 (one per page)

Sources:
- Niche-relevant blogs (roofing trade blogs, home improvement sites)
- Local news sites
- Business article directories
- Industry association resources
- Partner/supplier websites
- Guest posts on relevant small publications

Cost benchmark: ~$35 per link from private link networks or link fulfillment services

**Category 2: Local Authority Links**

Purpose: Prove your business is a legitimate, trusted community member

These are far more powerful than Category 1 links for local rankings.

#### Local Authority Link Sources (Priority Order):

**1. Chamber of Commerce Memberships (Highest Priority)**
- Join your LOCAL chamber first (~$200-300/year)
- Then join chambers in nearby cities you want to rank in
- Benefits:
  - High-trust domain (.org, government-adjacent)
  - Google specifically recognizes chamber links as legitimacy signals
  - Also recognized by AI systems as proof of established business
  - Often includes directory listing + profile page
  - Case study: Personal injury attorney moved from position 8 to position 3 overnight after joining chambers

**2. Youth Sports Team Sponsorships**
- Sponsor local Little League, soccer, football teams
- Cost: $200-500/season typically
- Team websites link to sponsors
- Often covered by local news
- Strong local authority signal

**3. Charity and Community Events**
- Donate to local events, walks, fundraisers
- Most charity websites list donors with links
- Community newspapers often cover local business involvement

**4. City/County Government Directories**
- Many municipalities have approved contractor lists
- Apply for local contractor licensing where applicable
- These .gov links are extremely high authority

**5. Local Business Associations**
- Rotary Club, Lions Club, local trade associations
- Home Builders Association
- Realtors Association (affiliate memberships)
- Local roofing/contractor associations

**6. Manufacturer/Supplier Dealer Finders**
- GAF Roofing certified contractor finder
- CertainTeed ShingleMaster/SELECT ShingleMaster
- Owens Corning Preferred Contractor
- Lennox, Carrier, Trane dealer locators (HVAC)
- These are high-trust, industry-specific authority links

**7. Local Media Coverage**
- Press releases about community involvement
- Expert quotes for local news articles on storm damage, etc.
- "Expert contractor" features in local real estate sections

### Link Building Workflow — New Client

```
Week 1-2: Core 30 pages created
Week 2-4: Source 30 content validation links (one per page)
Month 1:  Submit to manufacturer dealer finder programs
Month 1:  Apply for BBB accreditation
Month 2:  Join local chamber of commerce
Month 2:  Apply to city contractor directory
Month 3:  Identify 2-3 local sponsorship opportunities
Ongoing: One new local authority link per month minimum
```

### Backlink Monitoring

Tools: Google Search Console (Links report), Ahrefs, Moz, SEMrush

Monitor for:
- New links acquired (verify they're indexed)
- Lost links (follow up with linking sites)
- Toxic/spammy links (disavow if necessary)
- Competitor links (replicate high-quality ones)

### Toxic Link Management

If site has history of spammy links:
```
1. Export all backlinks from Google Search Console + Ahrefs
2. Identify spammy patterns: link farms, PBNs, adult/casino sites
3. Attempt manual removal (contact site owners)
4. Create disavow file for remaining toxic links
5. Submit disavow file via Google Search Console
6. Monitor for recovery (can take 2-3 months)
```

## 10. E-E-A-T & Authority Building

### Understanding E-E-A-T

Google's quality rater guidelines define four quality dimensions:

- **Experience** — First-hand, real-world experience with the topic
- **Expertise** — Demonstrated knowledge and skills in the field
- **Authoritativeness** — Recognition from others in the industry/community
- **Trustworthiness** — Accuracy, honesty, safety, and reliability

For local contractors, E-E-A-T translates to concrete, measurable signals.

### Experience Signals

**Content that demonstrates real experience:**
- Before/after project photos (YOUR projects, not stock)
- Case studies with specific details ("We replaced 47 squares of shingle on a 1980s ranch home in Katy...")
- Jobsite photos with geolocation data
- Team member photos doing actual work
- Videos of your team on-site
- Specific material and technique details that only a practitioner would know
- Local knowledge (mentioning area-specific challenges like Houston clay soil, Texas heat)

**What NOT to do:**
- Stock photos (reverse image searchable — Google knows)
- Generic descriptions that could apply to any business
- Content that reads like it was written by someone who's never done the work

### Expertise Signals

**Certifications and credentials to display prominently:**
- Manufacturer certifications (GAF Master Elite, CertainTeed SELECT, Owens Corning Preferred)
- State contractor license number
- Insurance (General Liability + Workers Comp) certificates
- Better Business Bureau rating and accreditation
- Industry association memberships (NRCA, RCAT for roofers; ACCA for HVAC)
- Years in business (specific: "serving Houston since 2008")
- Number of projects completed ("2,000+ roofs installed")

**Schema for expertise:**
```json
{
  "@type": "RoofingContractor",
  "hasCredential": [
    {
      "@type": "EducationalOccupationalCredential",
      "name": "GAF Master Elite Contractor",
      "credentialCategory": "Contractor Certification"
    }
  ]
}
```

### Authoritativeness Signals

**External recognition sources:**
- Chamber of commerce membership (explicitly signals community authority)
- Manufacturer preferred/elite contractor status
- BBB A+ rating and accreditation
- Industry awards and recognitions
- Media mentions (local news, trade publications)
- Speaking at local events or trade shows
- Guest posting on industry publications
- Testimonials from recognizable local organizations

**Building topical authority through content:**
- Having a dedicated page for EVERY service in your category
- Supporting content answering every major question about your trade
- Being the most comprehensive resource for your trade in your city
- When Google sees you have thorough coverage of all roofing topics → topical authority = higher rankings

### Trustworthiness Signals

**On-site trust signals:**
- [ ] Physical address displayed (not just a PO box)
- [ ] Phone number in header (click-to-call)
- [ ] Google Maps embed showing real location
- [ ] Review widget displaying real Google reviews
- [ ] License number displayed (with link to state verification)
- [ ] Insurance documentation mentioned
- [ ] Privacy policy page
- [ ] Terms of service page
- [ ] Secure HTTPS with valid SSL certificate
- [ ] Professional photos (real, not stock)
- [ ] Real team member names and photos
- [ ] Owner bio/about page with personal story

**Off-site trust signals:**
- Active GBP with recent reviews and responses
- Consistent NAP across all citations
- Super citations on verified platforms
- BBB accreditation
- Positive review velocity (steady new reviews over time)

## 11. AEO / GEO — AI Search Engine Optimization

### The AI Search Landscape (2025-2026)

AI search engines that recommend local businesses:
- **ChatGPT** — Browses web, checks business consistency, generates recommendations "like a friend"
- **Perplexity** — Heavy real-time web crawling, cites sources, local business results
- **Google Gemini / AI Overviews** — Integrated into Google Search, pulls from GBP and web
- **Copilot (Microsoft)** — Powered by Bing, uses Bing for Business data
- **Siri / Apple Maps** — Voice AI, uses Apple Maps and Yelp data
- **Alexa** — Uses Bing and Yelp primarily
- **BMW/Mercedes navigation AI** — Uses specific map provider data (Bing, HERE Maps)

### Why AI Recommendations Are More Valuable Than Search Rankings

When ChatGPT recommends a contractor, it feels like:
- A trusted friend's personal recommendation
- An expert opinion from a knowledgeable advisor
- Third-party validation (not paid advertising)

This creates a dramatically different trust level than a ranked search result. Conversion rates from AI recommendations are estimated to be 3-5x higher than traditional organic clicks.

### GEO — Generative Engine Optimization

Correctly executed SEO covers approximately 80% of GEO. The remaining 20% is AI-specific:

**The 20% AI-Specific Optimizations:**

1. **Schema markup** — AI systems parse schema more heavily than traditional Google because they are less efficient at inferring meaning from prose
2. **Business consistency across ALL platforms** — AI checks consistency just like Google, but across more sources
3. **Super citations on AI-training platforms** — Bing, Apple Maps, Yelp, and similar platforms are in AI training datasets
4. **Explicit service declarations** — AI systems need to FIND explicit proof you offer a specific service; dedicated service pages are critical
5. **Brand mentions in multiple contexts** — AI recommendations are based on pattern recognition from multiple independent sources mentioning your brand positively
6. **The 250 Authority Content Protocol** — Building content across platforms ensures your brand exists in AI training data

### How ChatGPT Recommends Local Contractors

When a user asks ChatGPT: "Who is the best roofing contractor in Houston?"

ChatGPT evaluates:
1. **Web search results** — Which contractors appear prominently in Google search?
2. **Business profile consistency** — Do Google, Yelp, BBB, and Bing show consistent information?
3. **Review presence and quality** — Do multiple review sources show positive sentiment?
4. **Schema markup** — Are services explicitly labeled for AI parsing?
5. **Community mentions** — Is this business mentioned on local news, community sites, blogs?
6. **Verification marks** — Is this business listed on verified/authoritative platforms?

Strategy: Everything that makes Google trust you also makes ChatGPT trust you.

### AEO-Specific Content Tactics

**1. Direct Answer Content**
Write content in the format AI systems prefer to extract and cite:
- Start service page sections with a clear, direct statement
- BAD: "At ABC Roofing, we have been providing amazing services..."
- GOOD: "ABC Roofing provides roof replacement services in Houston, TX, including full tear-off and installation of asphalt shingle, metal, and TPO flat roof systems."

**2. FAQ Optimization for AI**
FAQ content in Question-Answer format is heavily used by AI:
- Use the exact question phrasing people use
- Answer completely and specifically in 2-4 sentences
- Add FAQPage schema to every FAQ section
- AI systems extract Q&A pairs directly for featured snippets and AI answers

**3. Entity-Relationship Content**
Help AI systems understand what your business is, where it is, and what it does:
- "ABC Roofing LLC is a Houston-based roofing contractor specializing in..."
- "Located at [address], we serve [list of cities and neighborhoods]"
- "Our services include [explicit list matching GBP services]"

**4. Structured Comparison Content**
AI systems love tables and structured comparisons:
- "Asphalt vs. Metal Roofing: Which Is Right for Houston Homes?"
- "Cost Breakdown: What Factors Affect Roof Replacement Pricing?"
- Tables, bullet lists, and structured content is parsed more accurately

**5. Authoritative Citations Within Content**
Link to authoritative sources from your content:
- State licensing board
- Manufacturer warranty information
- Industry standards organizations (NRCA, ASHRAE for HVAC)
- Government building code resources

This signals to AI that your content is grounded in real, verifiable information.

### Voice Search Optimization

Voice search (Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant) uses different signals:
- Conversational query structure ("Who fixes roofs near me?")
- Local business data from Apple Maps, Yelp, Google
- Super citations on these platforms are critical
- FAQ content that answers natural language questions
- Clear NAP on every page (voice AI extracts this for "call" actions)

**Voice search content tips:**
- Write FAQ answers in conversational language
- Include questions with natural phrasing ("how much does it cost" not "pricing information")
- Ensure click-to-call is prominently placed (voice search leads to phone calls)

### Perplexity Optimization

Perplexity searches the live web and cites sources. To rank in Perplexity:
- Rank in Google (Perplexity heavily uses Google results)
- Have content that directly answers the query
- Be cited from other reputable sources
- Have accurate, up-to-date information (Perplexity checks current data)
- Use specific statistics and facts (Perplexity prefers citable data)

## 13. The 250 Authority Content Protocol

### What Is the 250 Authority Content Protocol?

The 250 Authority Content Protocol is a forward-looking AEO strategy: produce at least **250 high-quality, diverse pieces of content** distributed across multiple platforms and formats. The goal is to ensure that when AI language models retrain their data (which happens periodically), your brand is well-represented across the internet as a legitimate, knowledgeable authority in your trade.

When someone asks ChatGPT "Who is the best roofer in Houston?", ChatGPT recommends businesses based on:
1. Pattern recognition from its training data (what sources mentioned which businesses positively?)
2. Live web search results (what ranks in Google right now?)
3. Consistency of business information across platforms

The 250 Protocol targets #1 — ensuring your business appears repeatedly, positively, and in diverse contexts throughout the internet so that AI models learn to associate your brand with authority.

- One blog post or one social profile = noise
- 10-20 pieces = barely a footprint

## 14. Local Rank Tracking & the Top 3% Metric

### The Local Rank Map Explained

Traditional rank tracking shows your position for a keyword at ONE point — your city center. But customers search from all over your service area. A rank map shows your position at **169 points** distributed across your entire service area (a 13x13 grid).

**What you see:**
```
[Green]  = Positions 1-3 (visible, gets clicks)
[Yellow] = Positions 4-10 (partially visible)
[Orange] = Positions 11-20 (largely invisible)
[Red]    = Positions 21+ (effectively invisible)
```

**The Top 3% metric**: What percentage of the 169 grid points are green?
- 0-20% = New/struggling site — prioritize Core 30 content
- 20-40% = Building relevance — continue Core 30 + start supporting content
- 40-60% = Topical authority emerging — add supporting content aggressively
- 60-80% = Strong authority — shift to geographic expansion
- 80%+   = Dominant — maintain + expand to new service areas

### Rank Map Tools

| Tool | Cost | Features |
|------|------|----------|
| Lead Snap | ~$50-100/mo | Grid rank tracking, GBP insights, citation finder |
| Local Falcon | ~$30-80/mo | SERP grid, competitor comparison |
| BrightLocal | ~$29-79/mo | Citations + rank tracking + audit |
| Whitespark | ~$20-100/mo | Citation building + local rank tracker |
| GeoRanker | Custom | Enterprise local rank tracking |

### How to Run a Rank Map Analysis

```
1. Set up rank tracking in your tool of choice (Lead Snap recommended)
2. Enter:
   - Business GBP listing
   - Primary keyword (e.g., "roofing contractor")
   - Service area (city or radius)
   - Grid size (13x13 = 169 points recommended)
3. Run scan (takes 5-15 minutes)
4. Screenshot results — this is your baseline
5. Record:
   - Top 3% (green percentage)
   - Dominant area (where are you strongest?)
   - Weak areas (where are you red?)
   - Competitor scores for same keyword
6. Compare against competitors:
   - Run same scan for top 3 competitors
   - Note their Top 3% scores
   - Identify the gap to close
```

## 15. Geographic Expansion Strategy

### When to Start Geographic Expansion

**Only expand geographically when:**
1. Core 30 is complete and indexed
2. Rank map shows you are AT or ABOVE the topical authority threshold
3. You are ranking for 60%+ of your primary service area in top 3
4. All existing pages have external backlinks

Building location pages before establishing topical authority = spreading thin without impact. Google must first trust that you are the authority on roofing in Houston proper before it will accept your authority for Katy, Sugarland, or The Woodlands.

## 17. Review Strategy & Reputation Management

### Why Reviews Are Critical (Beyond Obvious)

Reviews affect:
1. **Local pack rankings** — Review count and rating are direct ranking factors
2. **Click-through rate** — Star ratings in search results dramatically increase clicks
3. **Conversion rate** — Reviews on site increase form submissions and calls
4. **AI recommendations** — ChatGPT/Perplexity assess review sentiment across platforms
5. **Trust** — 88% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations

**Review velocity matters** — 20 reviews from 3 years ago is worse than 20 reviews in the past 3 months. Google values RECENCY.

### The Review Acquisition System

**Step 1: Timing (When to Ask)**
Best moments to request a review:
- Immediately after job completion (payment received)
- When customer expresses satisfaction in-person
- During follow-up call 2-3 days after completion
- Never before work is complete
- Never when there is an open dispute or complaint

**Step 2: The Ask (How to Request)**

In-person script:
```
"[Customer name], I'm really glad we could help you with your roof. 
We're a small local business and reviews mean everything to us. 
Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? 
I can text you a direct link right now — it takes less than 2 minutes."
```

Text message template:
```
Hi [Name], it was great working with you! If you have a moment, 
a Google review would mean the world to us. Here's the direct link:
[short Google review link]
Thank you — the ABC Roofing team
```

Email follow-up template:
```
Subject: [Name], how did we do?

Hi [Name],

Thank you for choosing ABC Roofing for your [service].
We hope you're thrilled with the results.

As a small Houston roofing company, we rely on word-of-mouth 
and reviews to keep growing. If you're happy with our work, 
could you share a quick Google review?

[LEAVE A REVIEW BUTTON → direct link]

It takes less than 2 minutes and helps other Houston homeowners 
find a contractor they can trust.

With gratitude,
[Owner Name]
ABC Roofing
```

**Step 3: Creating the Direct Review Link**
```
1. Go to your Google Business Profile
2. Click "Share review form"
3. Copy the direct link
4. Use bit.ly or similar to shorten
5. Use this link in all review requests
```

**Step 4: Automation**
Set up automated review requests:
- Trigger: Job marked complete in CRM
- Action: Auto-send review request SMS + email after 24 hours
- Follow-up: If no review after 5 days, second gentle request

## 23. Common Mistakes & How to Fix Them

### Mistake 1: Homepage Title Tag Is "Home" or Brand Name Only

**Problem:** 60%+ of local businesses use "Home" or just their brand name as the homepage title tag. This wastes the single most valuable on-page SEO element.

**Fix:**
```
Old: Home | ABC Roofing
New: Houston Roofing Contractor — Same-Day Estimates | ABC Roofing
```

**Impact:** Can move rankings 5-10 positions within 2-4 weeks of fix.

### Mistake 2: One Page for All Services

**Problem:** A single "Services" page listing all offerings gives Google no way to rank you for individual service entities. You get zero rankings for specific searches.

**Fix:** Build the Core 30 — one dedicated page per service entity. Each page is a separate ranking opportunity.

**Impact:** Largest single structural improvement available. Can multiply ranking footprint 10-20x.

### Mistake 3: Collapsed/Accordion FAQ Sections

**Problem:** FAQ sections built with collapse/expand JavaScript are invisible to Googlebot, which cannot click to expand them.

**Fix:** Either make all FAQ content visible by default (no JavaScript toggle needed) OR restructure as an open Q&A section. Apply FAQPage schema to the visible content.

**Impact:** Can unlock significant hidden content that was being ignored by Google.

### Mistake 4: Identical Content Across Multiple Location Pages

**Problem:** Building 20 city pages by swapping one city name for another. Google detects near-duplicate content and suppresses all pages.

**Fix:** Each location page needs unique content that specifically references that location (local landmarks, area-specific characteristics, local permit info, neighborhood names).

**Impact:** Fixes duplicate content suppression and allows each location page to rank independently.

### Mistake 5: GBP Categories Don't Match Website

**Problem:** GBP has "Roofing Contractor" as primary category but homepage title says "Home Improvement Company." This mismatch confuses Google's relevance signals.

**Fix:** GBP primary category = homepage title tag entity. Every GBP category should have a matching page on the website.

**Impact:** Can improve map pack rankings significantly within 30-60 days.

### Mistake 6: NAP Inconsistency Across Citations

**Problem:** Phone number formatted differently (713-555-1234 vs. (713) 555-1234), address abbreviated differently (St. vs. Street), business name varies. This fragmentation reduces trust.

**Fix:**
1. Create master NAP document with exact format for all elements
2. Run citation audit (BrightLocal)
3. Correct all inconsistencies to match master NAP
4. Use master NAP document for ALL future submissions

**Impact:** Slow but steady trust improvement. Often unlocks significant ranking improvement for older businesses with citation histories.

### Mistake 7: No Schema on Service Pages

**Problem:** Service pages have no schema markup. Google (and especially AI systems) cannot confirm what service the page represents without explicit labeling.

**Fix:** Add Service schema to every service page. Add LocalBusiness schema to homepage. Validate all schema to zero errors.

**Impact:** Particularly impactful for AI search visibility. Can improve AI recommendation frequency within 30-60 days of implementation.

### Mistake 8: Using "Best" in Title Tags

**Problem:** "Best Houston Roofing Contractor" — the word "best" is treated as a spam signal by modern Google. Sites using superlatives in titles are flagged as potentially manipulative.

**Fix:** Remove all superlatives (best, top, #1, greatest, premier, leading). Replace with specific differentiators:
```
Old: Best Houston Roofing Contractor | ABC Roofing
New: Houston Roofing Contractor — GAF Certified | ABC Roofing
```

### Mistake 9: Building Location Pages Before Topical Authority

**Problem:** Building 30 location pages before having topical authority established. Google rejects these as thin content because it doesn't yet trust the site.

**Fix:** Follow the rank map decision tree — build Core 30 + supporting content first. Only expand geographically when rank map shows topical authority established (Top 3% at or above competitor threshold).

### Mistake 10: Neglecting GBP Activity

**Problem:** GBP profile set up once and then dormant. Google's algorithm treats activity as a signal — dormant profiles rank lower than active ones.

**Fix:** Automate GBP posting (1x/week minimum) and photo uploads (4x/month). Set up review request automation. Respond to all reviews within 24 hours.

**Impact:** Steady improvement in GBP visibility. Photos and posts also show up directly in the map pack results.

### Mistake 11: Desktop-First Design

**Problem:** Designing the website on a desktop browser, then checking mobile at the end. Googlebot crawls the MOBILE version — a design that breaks on mobile gets the broken version indexed.

**Fix:** Use Chrome DevTools mobile emulator throughout the design process. Use Google Search Console URL Inspection → "View Crawled Page" to see exactly what Googlebot sees.

### Mistake 12: No External Links to Individual Service Pages

**Problem:** Building 30 service pages but only getting backlinks to the homepage. Service pages with no external links are seen as low-authority.

**Fix:** Source at least one external link to each Core 30 page. This is the minimum link strategy for a new site build.
